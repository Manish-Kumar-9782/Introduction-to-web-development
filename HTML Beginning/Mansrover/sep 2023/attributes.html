Sonia Gandhi's Letter To PM Sparks Controversy: After BJP's 'Unaware
Of Traditions' Barb, Congress Hits Back

New Delhi: Ever since the Centre announced a special session of Parliament which is scheduled to begin on September 18, the Opposition and the BJP have been sparring over the issue. Earlier in the day, Congress leader Sonia Gandhi wrote to Prime Minister Narendra Modi over the agenda of the proposed special Parliament session.

Highlighting the absence of a declared agenda for the upcoming special parliament session commencing on September 18, she presented a list of nine topics, urging discussions on them. The subjects encompassed matters such as Center-state relations, communalism, the situation in Manipur, and the border dispute with China.

"You have convened a special five-day session of the Parliament beginning 18 September 2023. I must point out that this Special Session has been convened without any consultation with other political parties. None of us have any idea of its agenda," the Congress leader said.

A few hours later Parliamentary Affairs Minister Pralhad Joshi responded to Congress leader Sonia Gandhi's letter to PM Modi, accusing the Congress MP of politicising the functioning of Parliament.

"The session has been called following tradition. Perhaps you are not paying attention to tradition. Before calling the Parliament session, there is never any discussion with political parties, nor are the issues discussed. After the calling of the session by the President and before commencement of the session, there is a meeting of the leaders of all the parties in which the people rising in the Parliament are discussed. Issues and work are discussed." the minister wrote.
